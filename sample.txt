University of Mary Washington
*****
Locations:
Double Drive
Welcome to the University of Mary Washington! You are
currently at the main entrance for the school.
+++
James Farmer Hall
This building is named after Dr. James Farmer and houses
the Departments of Mathematics, Computer Science, CPR,
Education and Asian Studies. It was built in 1941 and was
originally the library. An addition was completed in 1963.
+++
Campus Walk East
This sidewalk winds through the entire campus leading
you to all other locations on campus.
+++
Bell Tower
The tower houses a clock and is a monumental piece at UMW
built in 2007. The bell chimes twice a day at noon and 6pm.
+++
George Washington Hall
This building has all administrators and human resources.
It was completed in 1939.
+++
Combs Hall
Combs Hall contains the Department of English and Linguistics.
It was built in 1959.
+++
Campus Walk West
This is the main thoroughfare for the west side of campus.
+++
For Five Coffee
Located in the HCC, For Five Coffee will be offering sustainably
sourced, locally roasted coffees, organic loose-leaf teas, and
a plant-forward vegetarian and wellness-centered menu that
includes gluten-free selections.
+++
Lee Hall
Lee Hall houses the bookstore, student services and the health center.
+++
Bookstore
The bookstore is for all at UMW. Students can find textbooks and
supplies. Everyone can find gear to represent our beloved school!
+++
Monroe Hall
Monroe Hall is one of the three original buildings on campus. It was
built in 1911. It houses the History, Geography, and Political
Science Departments.
+++
Monroe Fountain
This fountain is central to the original three buildings of campus.
Many students enjoy a refreshing feet dip in spring. You never
know what you might find walking by...towering bubbles?
+++
Simpson Library
The library is in its second location on campus. It was built in 1987
as a replacement to Trinkle (now James Farmer) Library. This library
then had space for many student amenities including study spaces
and larger book collections. If you need a quiet working space, visit
the treehouses on the third floor!
+++
Amphitheater
A historic, 1,600 person capacity outdoor venue built in 1923,
it features a crescent-shaped stage, Greek Doric columns, and tiered bench seating,
hosting concerts, graduations, and theatrical events after a major 2018 restoration!
